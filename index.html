Interactive Grounding for Multi-Robot Coordination using Affordance-Based Hypergraphs

Abstract

Imbuing physical understanding in Vision-Language Models (VLMs), commonly referred to as grounding, has been shown to improve their planning ability in robotic environments. However, providing grounding in multi-robot settings necessitates VLMs not only understanding different robot capabilities, but also how different robots interact with the world. While prior work in multi-robot settings has shown providing feedback on failed plans to VLMs improves grounding, they fail to reason over multiple failure modalities, fundamentally limiting their ability for adaptive planning. Addressing this lack of grounding, we present an affordance-based hypergraph for interactively grounding VLMs by reasoning about different causes of skill failure. The hypergraph unifies object affordances, robot capabilities, and robot skills, effectively capturing what a robot can do based on its capabilities and what is available in the environment. Using the hypergraph improves grounding by providing specific feedback when task failures occur, due to poor VLM grounding or capability failures, and executing or recommending other potentially viable skills after skill failure. Simulation results show Interactive Grounding improves success rate and infeasible task detection by more than 3x over prior state-of-the-art methods and hardware demonstrations showcase Interactive Grounding adapting real robots to an object with uncertain mass.

Standard Warehouse Environment

The objective is to move the two blocks on the table to the drop off location. In this video, the gripper capability of the Franka on the table has failed. Interactive Grounding identifies the capability failure and successfully updates the the plan such that the drone is no longer used, since the objects may be too heavy for it to carry. The video is 4x speed.

Large Warehouse Environment

The objective is to move all boxes to the drop off locations. The UR5 on the far table has lost its move capability, the nearest quadruped has lost its walk capability, and the middle Franka has lost its gripper capability. Two of the large boxes are immovable and the small box nearest the UR5 on the far table cannot be moved since it is outside the reach of Franka. Interactive Grounding successfully adapts to the robot capability failures and correctly identifies all immovable objects. The video is 4x speed.

Hardware Demonstration

The Interactive Grounding framework is deployed on Franka and UR5 manipulators. Interactive Grounding first attempts to cooperatively pick up and move the box, but the object is too heavy to do so. Querying the hypergraph, Interactive Grounding identifies a cooperative push skill as a viable skill, executes it, and successfully moves the box to the white table. This is done with only a single VLM call.
